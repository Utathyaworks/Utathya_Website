About Me

Principal Research Scientist — Google DeepMind · San Francisco

I lead a compact team focused on NeRF & neural scene representation. At Google I've shipped features and research spanning Glass, Lens Blur, HDR+, VR, Portrait Mode, Portrait Light, Maps, and Shopping — balancing deep research with product-quality engineering.

I hold a PhD from UC Berkeley (advisor: Jitendra Malik) and received the PAMI Young Researcher Award. My core interests are multimodal learning, LLMs & agents, and visual scene understanding.

Research Interests

Neural Rendering
Developing neural representations for 3D scenes, including NeRF and its extensions for high-quality view synthesis and scene understanding.
3D Reconstruction
Creating methods to recover 3D structure from images, focusing on shape, motion, and appearance estimation from visual data.
Computational Photography
Advancing techniques in HDR imaging, deblurring, and image enhancement using both traditional and learning-based approaches.
Generative AI
Exploring diffusion models and other generative techniques for creating and editing 3D content and images.
Computer Vision
Fundamental problems in vision including object detection, segmentation, and understanding physical properties from images.
Machine Learning
Developing new architectures and training methods for vision tasks, with focus on efficiency and generalization.
